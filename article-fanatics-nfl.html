NFL Cards Shake-Up: Fanatics Takes Over the License for 2026 — What It Means for Collectors

JM
James Mitchell
Senior Sports Card Analyst
·March 5, 2026·6 min read·✓ Expert Verified

The NFL trading card world has officially entered a new era. As of March 2026, Fanatics Collectibles — through its Topps brand — holds the exclusive NFL trading card license, ending Panini's long run as the sole producer of officially licensed NFL cards in the United States.

This is the single biggest licensing shift in football card history since Upper Deck and Donruss competed for the market decades ago. For collectors, it raises immediate questions: What happens to my Panini cards? Will Topps NFL cards be better? And what should I buy — or avoid — right now?

Why Did the License Change?

Fanatics, the sports merchandise giant that acquired Topps in 2021, has been methodically securing major league licenses across all sports. The company struck deals with MLB first, then the NBA, and now the NFL — completing a clean sweep of America's four major professional sports leagues (the NHL license follows shortly after).

Panini's NFL contract expired, and Fanatics outbid them for renewal. The transition gives Topps — a brand with deep football card history from the 1950s through the early 2000s — its first NFL license in over two decades.

What Happens to Panini NFL Cards?

Here's what collectors need to understand about Panini's existing NFL catalog:

🔑 Key Facts About the Panini-to-Topps Transition

  • Existing Panini cards retain their value — Licensing changes don't invalidate previously produced cards; they remain legitimate collectibles
  • Last Panini NFL season was 2025 — Any Panini NFL product released before March 2026 is the final generation; "last of an era" cards sometimes carry nostalgic premiums
  • Panini Prizm Football 2025 — The final Prizm football set; historically popular with collectors and likely to hold strong as the last chromium Prizm NFL product
  • Patrick Mahomes and key star cards on Panini — Major stars' Panini rookie cards are unaffected; their PSA and BGS grades still carry full market value
  • No new Panini NFL sets after 2025 — Future NFL rookie cards will only be available from Topps/Fanatics

What to Expect from Topps NFL Cards

Based on what Fanatics/Topps has done with their NBA and MLB licenses, collectors can expect:

  • Topps Series 1 NFL Football — A base flagship product with hobby and retail versions across major retailers
  • Topps Chrome NFL — The premium chromium product; the spiritual successor to Panini Prizm for football
  • Topps Finest NFL — Ultra-premium autograph and relic-heavy product
  • Topps Stadium Club NFL — Photography-forward premium set beloved by artistic collectors
  • Digital integration — Fanatics' ownership means tighter digital-physical card integration through their platforms

Investment Angle: What Should Collectors Do Now?

The licensing shift creates several actionable opportunities and risks for collectors and investors:

  • Buy strong Panini Prizm rookies — The 2020–2025 Prizm rookie cards of stars like Justin Jefferson, Ja'Marr Chase, and CJ Stroud may carry added collector nostalgia as "the Panini era" recedes
  • Watch Topps NFL first-year premiums — Just as Topps' first NBA set gained historic significance, Topps' first NFL set will likely be viewed as a landmark product; consider case breaks at launch
  • Don't panic-sell Panini — There's no reason to dump quality graded Panini NFL cards; their market is unchanged
  • Watch 2026 NFL rookie class timing — The first Topps NFL rookies (from the 2026 Draft) will be historically significant; position accordingly
"Panini built one of the most beloved football card brands in hobby history with Prizm. Topps has massive shoes to fill. But if their NBA transition is any guide, they'll come out swinging with quality products — and first-year Topps NFL rookies will be highly collectible for generations." — James Mitchell, ACT Collectibles

The Bottom Line

Change is rarely bad for collectors in the long run. Competition, new product designs, and the excitement of "first-year" products historically inject energy into the hobby. The Fanatics/Topps NFL era begins now — and whether you're a casual collector or serious investor, it's worth paying close attention to the first wave of products.
