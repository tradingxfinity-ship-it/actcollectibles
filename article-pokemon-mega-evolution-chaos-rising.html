Pokémon TCG: Mega Evolution — Chaos Rising Full Set Guide: Every Chase Card, New Rarity, and What to Buy Before Launch

SR
Sarah Rodriguez
TCG Market Editor · ACT Collectibles

The fourth expansion in the Mega Evolution block — Chaos Rising — drops May 22, 2026, and it's bringing some of the most anticipated cards of the year. Over 120 cards, four brand-new Mega Evolution Pokémon ex debuts, a never-before-seen Mega Attack Rare rarity with retro katakana attack text, and a League Battle Deck headlined by Mega Lucario ex. Prerelease events ran May 9–17 at local game stores. Here's everything you need to know before the full launch.

The New Mega Pokémon ex Debuts

Chaos Rising introduces four Mega Evolution Pokémon ex that have never appeared in this block: Mega Greninja ex, Mega Pyroar ex, Mega Floette ex, and Mega Dragalge ex. Each gets full-art treatment in the new Mega Attack Rare rarity, which features retro katakana attack text borrowed from the original Japanese card design language — a deep cut for long-time collectors who remember the pre-English print era.

Mega Greninja ex is the consensus chase card of this set. Greninja has been a fan-favourite since XY era, and a Mega Evolution treatment with full-art design and high-rarity status makes this one of the most desirable Pokémon cards of 2026. Pre-launch buylist prices at major dealers are already targeting $80–$120 for a raw copy, with graded estimates significantly higher.

Products Available at Launch

The product lineup for Chaos Rising is one of the strongest of the Mega Evolution block. Standard Elite Trainer Boxes include nine packs plus a full-art Fennekin promo, sleeves, energies, and accessories. The Pokémon Center exclusive ETB upgrades to eleven packs and two Fennekin promos — worth sourcing if you can access the Pokémon Center directly. The Mega Lucario ex League Battle Deck is the most interesting product for competitive players and set collectors alike: a complete 60-card ready-to-play deck featuring Mega Lucario ex as the headliner. These preconstructed decks frequently contain exclusive cards not found in standard booster packs.

The Mega Zygarde ex Premium Collection rounds out the launch lineup — a premium-priced bundle with promo cards, oversized card, booster packs, and a pin. Zygarde's iconic 100% Forme makes this a strong aesthetic choice for display collectors even if the investment case is softer than core set pulls.

The Mega Attack Rare: What It Means for Collectors

The introduction of the Mega Attack Rare rarity is the most significant structural addition to the Mega Evolution block yet. The retro katakana attack text is a deliberate nod to original Japanese Pokémon TCG design — and it immediately creates a new category of cards that cross appeal between modern collectors and vintage Pokémon enthusiasts. Pull rates haven't been officially published yet, but based on regional prerelease data, collectors report approximately one Mega Attack Rare per two Elite Trainer Boxes, making them meaningfully scarce without being lottery-level rare.

For investors, new rarity introductions in Pokémon sets almost always create a short-term premium window in the first 4–6 weeks post-release. After supply catches up, prices typically correct 30–50% from launch peaks. The sweet spot for buying Mega Attack Rares as investments is 6–8 weeks post-release when that correction has happened — then hold for 12–18 months as set supply exhausts.
