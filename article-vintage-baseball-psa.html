PSA's Most Graded Vintage Baseball Cards — And What They're Worth Today

MP
Maria Perez
Vintage Cards Specialist
·February 20, 2025·10 min read·✓ Expert Verified

PSA's population reports are a treasure trove of data for any serious collector. They tell us not just how many cards have been graded, but which cards collectors care about most — because nobody pays $20+ per card to grade something they don't value. Looking at PSA's most-graded vintage baseball cards gives us a fascinating window into what the hobby truly prizes.

Here's our breakdown of the most submitted vintage baseball cards to PSA, what their populations look like, and what they're realistically selling for in 2025.

1. 1980 Topps Rickey Henderson RC — The All-Time King

No vintage baseball card has been sent to PSA more times than the 1980 Topps Rickey Henderson Rookie Card (#482). With well over 41,000 PSA-graded copies in the population report, Henderson's RC is the defining benchmark of modern vintage collecting.

Why so many? The Henderson RC sits in the sweet spot of accessibility and significance. Packs from 1980 survive in large numbers, making it easy to find raw copies at shows and flea markets. But the card is also genuinely important — Henderson is the all-time stolen base king and a first-ballot Hall of Famer.

⚾ 1980 Topps Rickey Henderson RC — Current Market Values

  • Raw (ungraded): $15–$60 depending on condition
  • PSA 7 (NM): $80–$120
  • PSA 8 (NM-MT): ~$250
  • PSA 9 (Mint): ~$2,400
  • PSA 10 (Gem Mint): $25,000–$50,000+ (extremely rare; only a handful exist)

The massive PSA 9-to-10 price gap on this card illustrates why gem mint vintage cards command such extraordinary premiums. Centering issues plague this print run, making a PSA 10 essentially a unicorn.

2. 1989 Upper Deck Ken Griffey Jr. Star Rookie RC — Most Graded Modern Card

The 1989 Upper Deck Ken Griffey Jr. (#1) is arguably the most important baseball card produced since World War II. It launched the modern era of premium card collecting, introduced collectors to Upper Deck's high-quality card stock, and features the kid who would become baseball's most beloved player of his generation.

PSA has graded approximately 10,100 copies, making it the most encapsulated modern-era baseball card in the hobby. Only about 4% of those have received a PSA 10 — a remarkably low gem rate for a card that was well-produced by the standards of its time.

⚾ 1989 Upper Deck Griffey Jr. RC — Current Market Values

  • Raw (ungraded): $20–$80
  • PSA 8: $150–$250
  • PSA 9: $600–$1,000
  • PSA 10: $8,000–$15,000+

3. 1975 Topps George Brett RC — The Quiet Giant

The 1975 Topps George Brett Rookie Card (#228) — technically a multi-player rookie card also featuring Robin Yount — has seen sustained submission volume year after year. PSA reports over 17,600 total graded copies, with around 2,000 new submissions in 2024 alone.

Brett is one of the most beloved players in Kansas City Royals history, a first-ballot Hall of Famer, and the star of the infamous "Pine Tar Game." His RC remains one of the most affordable gateway vintage cards that still carries real collector significance.

⚾ 1975 Topps George Brett RC — Current Market Values

  • Raw: $25–$100
  • PSA 8: $180–$280
  • PSA 9: $800–$1,400
  • PSA 10: $12,000–$20,000

4. 1952 Topps Mickey Mantle — The Hobby's Crown Jewel

No list of significant vintage baseball cards is complete without the 1952 Topps Mickey Mantle (#311). While not the most commonly graded card by volume — it's expensive and harder to find — it is universally recognized as the single most important post-war baseball card in American collecting history.

A PSA 9 copy sold for $5.2 million in 2022, setting an all-time record for any sports card at auction. Even mid-grade copies (PSA 5–6) regularly sell for $50,000–$200,000. This is the card every serious vintage collector aspires to own.

5. 1909–11 T206 Honus Wagner — The Holy Grail

The T206 Honus Wagner is the most famous, most valuable, and most talked-about card in all of American collecting. Produced during the tobacco card era of 1909–1911 and reportedly pulled from production because Wagner objected to his likeness being used to sell cigarettes, fewer than 60 authenticated copies are believed to exist.

A PSA 3 copy sold for $6.6 million in 2021. The card exists in a category entirely its own — less a collectible and more a historical artifact of American sports culture.

"Vintage baseball cards aren't just collectibles — they're time capsules. Every well-preserved Rickey Henderson or Mickey Mantle card that gets graded is a piece of American sports history being preserved for the next generation of collectors." — Maria Perez, ACT Collectibles

Tips for Getting Started with Vintage Baseball

If you're new to vintage baseball cards, here's how to get started without breaking the bank:

  • Start with mid-grade PSA 6–7 cards — You get the authentication and slab protection without paying the premium for 8s and 9s
  • Focus on Hall of Famers — HOF cards have proven floor value and long-term collector demand
  • Learn the key print years — 1952, 1955, 1963, 1967, 1975, 1980, and 1989 are all historically significant Topps/Upper Deck years
  • Buy the card, not just the grade — Eye appeal matters; two PSA 7 cards can look dramatically different
  • Shop card shows and estate sales — Ungraded vintage treasure can still be found by sharp-eyed collectors
