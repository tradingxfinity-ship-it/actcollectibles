Cooper Flagg Wins NBA Rookie of the Year — His Cards Just Hit a New Peak and the Topps NOW Record Is Staggering

JM
James Mitchell
Sports Card Editor · ACT Collectibles

Cooper Flagg arrived in the NBA as the most hyped rookie prospect in years — and he delivered. The Dallas Mavericks' 2025 No. 1 overall pick just won the 2026 NBA Rookie of the Year award, posting 21.0 points, 6.7 rebounds, and 4.5 assists per game across a full season. His cards, which had already been surging all year, spiked again on the announcement. And his Topps NOW ROTY card just broke the all-time Topps NOW print run record with an eye-opening 208,735 copies ordered.

The Season That Justified Every Cent

Flagg's rookie season was not a slow burn. He came out of Duke as one of the most polished two-way players to enter the NBA Draft in a generation, and he backed that up immediately. Across 70 games, he averaged 21.0 PPG, 6.7 RPG, 4.5 APG, 1.2 steals, and 0.9 blocks. He had a 50-point game in March that sent social media — and the card market — into overdrive. His efficiency numbers held up even as opponents adjusted to him in the second half of the season.

For card collectors, on-court performance is the core driver of value. Flagg delivered the narrative that every rookie card investor hopes for: immediate production, national media attention, and the kind of stat lines that make casual fans Google his name. That combination is what separates an elite rookie card market from a hype bubble.

Why the Topps Return Makes This Historically Significant

Flagg's rookie cards carry a layer of significance that goes beyond his performance: Topps returned to NBA basketball licensing for the first time in 16 years specifically for the 2025–26 season. After Panini held the exclusive NBA license since 2009, Topps secured a deal that made Flagg's class the first to have Topps Chrome basketball cards in over a decade. Collectors who have been in the hobby long enough to remember the Topps Chrome basketball era of the early 2000s know exactly what that means: the refractor parallels, the on-card autographs, the numbering conventions that Topps Chrome is famous for.

Flagg's first Topps Chrome on-card autograph is the most sought-after card of the rookie class. As of April 2026, those autos saw a 15% value increase following the ROTY announcement. PSA 10 graded copies are trading in the $800–$1,200 range depending on the parallel. His Gold Refractor /50 has crossed $4,000 at auction.

The Topps NOW Print Run Record

Topps NOW cards are print-on-demand releases tied to specific in-game moments or awards, available for a 24-hour window. The Cooper Flagg ROTY Topps NOW card recorded a print run of 208,735 copies — the highest ever for a Topps NOW card in any sport. For context, most notable Topps NOW cards print between 5,000 and 30,000 copies. A 200K+ print run is essentially unheard of. It reflects the crossover appeal of Flagg beyond the core hobby — casual fans ordering a card more as memorabilia than investment.

That distinction matters: high print-run Topps NOW cards are not investment vehicles. They're accessible collectibles. The numbered, limited parallel versions of Flagg's cards — particularly the 1/1 Superfractor and the /25 Orange — are where the real investment conversation happens.

Where the Market Stands Now

The card market for Flagg is stabilizing after the ROTY spike. Veteran investors who bought in during the preseason or early regular season have seen substantial gains. New buyers entering post-ROTY are paying near-peak prices and taking on more risk if Flagg's sophomore season underperforms. The smart money is watching his postseason performance — a deep playoff run would be the next major catalyst for another value increase.

💡
Collector Takeaway: If you own Flagg's low-numbered parallels or on-card autos, hold into the offseason and monitor his summer league activity. If you're buying in now, stick to PSA 10 graded base Chrome — the premium is most stable there. Avoid high-print Topps NOW cards as investments; they're collectibles, not assets.
