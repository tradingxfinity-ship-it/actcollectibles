Cooper Flagg's ROTY Topps NOW Card Just Set the All-Time Print Run Record: 208,735 Copies in 24 Hours

JM
James Mitchell
Sports Card Editor · ACT Collectibles

When Topps released the Cooper Flagg Rookie of the Year commemorative Topps NOW card, they weren't prepared for what happened next. In the standard 24-hour ordering window, collectors and fans ordered 208,735 copies — not just a Topps NOW record for basketball, but the highest print run ever recorded for any Topps NOW card in any sport. For context, a standout Topps NOW card typically prints between 5,000 and 30,000 copies. Flagg didn't just break the record — he obliterated it by a factor of seven.

What Is Topps NOW and Why Does This Matter?

Topps NOW is a print-on-demand product: Topps releases commemorative cards tied to specific in-game moments, milestones, or awards, available for exactly 24 hours on the Topps website. Whatever is ordered in that window becomes the official print run — every copy is numbered, and no additional copies are made. The format creates a real-time market where demand in the moment determines the card's future scarcity.

For reference, the previous NBA Topps NOW record was well under 50,000. Flagg's 208,735-copy ROTY card surpassed it by more than four times. Six of the top 10 highest NBA Topps NOW cards this season feature Cooper Flagg — a statistic that illustrates just how unprecedented his impact on the hobby has been in a single rookie year.

Price at ordering: Standard Topps NOW retail
Flagg's share of top-10 NBA Topps NOW cards: 6 of 10 this season

Investment Value: The Counter-Intuitive Reality

Here's what collectors need to understand: a 208,735-copy print run is not an investment card. It is a memento. The same dynamic that makes a 1/1 Superfractor incredibly valuable — extreme scarcity — works in reverse here. With over 200,000 copies in existence, the Flagg ROTY Topps NOW will always be affordable and never become rare.

That's not a criticism of the card — it's a different kind of collectible entirely. For fans who want to commemorate a historic rookie season at a low price point, the ROTY Topps NOW is perfect. For investors, this card is irrelevant except as a cultural data point: the demand that drove 208,000 orders in 24 hours is the same demand that makes Flagg's numbered, limited parallels and on-card autos extremely valuable. The mass market love for Flagg validates the investment thesis for his scarce cards.

What Topps Did After the Record

Topps responded to the record print run by announcing a limited numbered parallel version of the ROTY card — available separately and produced in a specific print run that hasn't been publicly confirmed. These numbered versions are where the actual investment conversation begins. Even if numbered to /1000 or /500, that's a fraction of the 208K base print run and will carry meaningful secondary market demand.

Topps also used the occasion to highlight the broader success of its return to NBA licensing. The Flagg-era rookie class has become the platform that proves Topps can compete at the level of the sport's biggest names after 16 years away from basketball. Whatever Flagg's career trajectory looks like from here, the inaugural Topps NBA era is permanently tied to his story.

💡
Collector Guidance: Buy the base ROTY Topps NOW if you're a fan — it's an affordable piece of hobby history. Do not buy it as an investment. If Topps releases a numbered parallel of the ROTY card at /500 or below, that has genuine collector value. Focus investment capital on Flagg's on-card Chrome autos and numbered refractors, not print-on-demand commemoratives.
