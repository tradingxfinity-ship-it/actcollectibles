MTG 2026: The Biggest Year in Magic History — Marvel in June, The Hobbit in August, Star Trek in November

SR
Sarah Rodriguez
TCG Market Editor · ACT Collectibles

Wizards of the Coast has never released more Standard sets in a single calendar year than it will in 2026. Seven sets in total — spanning original Magic worlds, beloved fantasy IPs, and some of pop culture's biggest franchises — make this the most ambitious MTG release schedule in the game's 30-plus-year history. From the return of Strixhaven to the debut of Marvel Super Heroes, here's every set, every date, and what collectors should be watching.

The Full 2026 MTG Release Calendar

Lorwyn Eclipsed (January 23, 2026) — The year kicked off with a return to Lorwyn, one of Magic's most beloved planes. The world of light and shadow makes its comeback with updated mechanics and gorgeous borderless art treatments that have already become the most-graded MTG cards of Q1 2026.

Teenage Mutant Ninja Turtles (February 10, 2026) — The TMNT crossover set delivered on its pop-culture appeal. Leonardo, Donatello, Michelangelo, and Raphael as commanders generated massive secondary market interest among non-traditional Magic collectors and proved that IP crossovers drive new money into the hobby.

Secrets of Strixhaven (April 2026) — A return to Arcavios and Strixhaven University, focusing on student life beyond the college walls. The most competitive-play-relevant set of the year, with five new legendary creatures for Commander formats.

Marvel Super Heroes (June 26) — The Biggest Set of the Year

Marvel Super Heroes is the headline release of 2026. Spider-Man, Iron Man, Thor, Black Panther, and dozens more Marvel characters translated into Magic card design — with Legendary Creature treatment for the most iconic heroes and villains. This is the set that non-Magic collectors are buying, which creates unusual market dynamics: high casual demand, strong aesthetic collectibility, and IP-driven value that doesn't depend on competitive play.

For investors, Marvel crossover cards follow the same pattern as the Lord of the Rings set from 2023 — initial FOMO-driven spike, correction, then a long-term floor set by IP licensing scarcity. The serialized one-of-one "The One Ring" style chase in this set is the 1/1 "Infinity Gauntlet" card. Expect enormous auction activity around it.

The Hobbit (August) and Star Trek (November)

The Hobbit set features Bilbo, Thorin, Gandalf, Gollum, and the full cast of the journey to the Lonely Mountain. Given the enormous collector overlap between fantasy literature enthusiasts and card collectors, this set has the broadest non-player appeal of any 2026 release. Sealed product is worth holding.

Star Trek in November caps the year — characters, stories, and ships from the entire 60-year franchise. The Trek fanbase has a deep tradition of collecting memorabilia, and crossover appeal into that community will drive demand well beyond the standard MTG player base.

Reality Fracture (October) — The Story Climax

Reality Fracture is the epic conclusion to the current arc of Magic's ongoing storyline — a showdown in the Meditation Realm. For competitive players and lore enthusiasts, this is the most mechanically significant in-universe set. Watch for powerful Commander pieces and tournament-playable cards that drive grading submissions.

💡
Investor Notes: Marvel borderless foil treatments and the 1/1 serialized chase are the best investment targets. Hobbit and Star Trek sets are worth buying sealed for long-term IP appreciation. Lorwyn Eclipsed borderless art cards from Q1 are already appreciating — still accessible at reasonable prices.
