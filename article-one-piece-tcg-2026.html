One Piece TCG in 2026: Why This Is One of the Fastest-Growing Collector Markets — and What's Worth Grading

SR
Sarah Rodriguez
TCG Market Editor · ACT Collectibles

Bandai's One Piece Card Game launched in English in 2022 and has spent the following four years proving that it can sustain and grow a serious collector market. In 2026, the game is one of the fastest-growing TCGs by graded card volume — driven by extremely popular characters, aggressive organized play support, and a competitive scene that keeps card values tied to actual gameplay. Here's what collectors and investors need to know right now.

The 2026 Product Landscape

Bandai has been releasing 2026 One Piece promotional products at a steady pace. The Tournament Pack 2026 Vol. 1 and Vol. 2 are the highest-profile collector releases of the year — limited availability packs given exclusively to top finishers at regional and national tournaments. These packs contain alternate art versions of popular cards and are the hardest to source legitimately, making them the most coveted collector items in the OP TCG ecosystem.

The Welcome Pack 2026 Vol. 1 is more accessible — distributed at official Bandai events to introduce new players to the game. These packs have limited print runs and feature unique promotional cards not available elsewhere, making them collector-relevant despite their introductory purpose.

2026 One Piece TCG Key Products:
Tournament Pack 2026 Vol. 1 — Top finisher exclusives, alternate arts
Tournament Pack 2026 Vol. 2 — Continued exclusive promo distribution
Winner Pack 2026 Vol. 2 — Winner-only promos from championship events
Welcome Pack 2026 Vol. 1 — New player promotional cards
Ongoing: Main set booster releases through Bandai's global distribution

CGC's Dominance in One Piece Grading

One of the most interesting market developments in 2026 is the emergence of CGC as the preferred grading company specifically for One Piece TCG cards. Unlike sports cards where PSA's market dominance creates near-mandatory PSA grading for maximum resale value, the One Piece community has coalesced around CGC — particularly for promo cards and tournament exclusives. CGC's faster turnaround and competitive pricing makes it well-suited for a game where metagame shifts can change card values quickly.

PSA is still grading One Piece cards and commands a premium on certain older ultra-rare cards from early sets, but CGC's growing presence means collectors should evaluate which grading company's registry population aligns better with their specific card category before submitting.

What Makes One Piece Cards Valuable

One Piece card values are driven by three factors: character popularity (Luffy, Zoro, Nami cards consistently outperform), competitive playability (tournament staples spike when a card becomes format-dominant), and exclusive distribution (tournament promos are the highest-value cards in the game). The interaction of all three is where peak value occurs — a competitively dominant card featuring a beloved character in exclusive tournament promo art is the Holy Grail of OP TCG collecting.

Early set cards — particularly from the OP-01 Romance Dawn set — have appreciated significantly as the collector base has grown. For Pokémon collectors looking to diversify into another TCG, One Piece has the most similar value structure: character-driven demand, competitive play layer, and a growing grading ecosystem creating price transparency.

The Fastest-Growing TCG Consideration

Collectiwiz's 2026 report identified One Piece as one of the two fastest-growing TCGs (alongside Lorcana) by secondary market transaction volume over the past 18 months. That growth reflects both a widening player base and increasing collector market sophistication. As more shops stock OP TCG and more tournaments run at the local game store level, the ecosystem that sustains long-term card value — a combination of player demand, collector interest, and organized play — is maturing rapidly.

💡
One Piece Collector Action Plan: Focus on Tournament Pack promos in high grade (CGC 9.5+). OP-01 Romance Dawn ultra-rares in PSA or CGC 10 are the best long-term hold. Watch the competitive meta — format staples spike when they win major tournaments. Grade with CGC for OP TCG specifically; the community is centered there.
