Pokémon Scarlet & Violet: Best Sets, Chase Cards & Complete Market Overview for 2025

SR
Sarah Rodriguez
TCG & Pokémon Markets Editor
·March 15, 2025·9 min read·✓ Expert Verified

The Scarlet & Violet era, which launched in March 2023, has fundamentally changed what Pokémon cards look like. The introduction of Illustration Rare and Special Illustration Rare cards — featuring breathtaking, full-art watercolor-style illustrations that break the traditional card border — has created an entirely new tier of premium collectible that the hobby had never seen before.

Two years in, the SV era has produced some of the most visually stunning cards in Pokémon's 25-year history, and the collector market has responded accordingly. Here's your complete guide to navigating the Scarlet & Violet card market in 2025.

What Makes Scarlet & Violet Cards Unique

Several structural changes define the SV era and explain why its premium cards command such high prices:

  • Illustration Rares (IR) — Full-bleed artwork that extends beyond the card border; stunning nature/lifestyle scenes featuring Pokémon in real-world contexts
  • Special Illustration Rares (SIR) — The highest rarity tier; often features both the Pokémon and their trainer in elaborate, gallery-quality artwork. These are the chase cards of every SV set.
  • Hyper Rares (Gold cards) — Gold-textured full-art cards featuring items, supporters, or Pokémon; perennially popular with collectors
  • Ex mechanic returns — The beloved ex mechanic from the original era returns, connecting new collectors to nostalgic players

Best Scarlet & Violet Sets to Collect

⚡ Ranked: Best SV Sets for Collectors (2023–2025)

  • Paldean Fates (January 2024) — Mass-market set with Shiny Pokémon; wildly popular for its accessible Shiny Charizard ex and broad appeal to younger collectors. The chase Shiny Charizard SIR commands $80–$200 in PSA 10.
  • Obsidian Flames (August 2023) — Home to the Tera Charizard ex, the single most sought-after SV-era card; PSA 10 copies trade at $200–$500. A must-have for any serious modern Pokémon collector.
  • Temporal Forces (March 2024) — Gorgeous artwork set with outstanding SIRs; Iron Leaves ex SIR and Walking Wake ex SIR are highlights
  • Stellar Crown (September 2024) — First set to feature Stellar-type Pokémon; Terapagos ex cards are the chase pieces with strong early market performance
  • Surging Sparks (November 2024) — Pikachu-heavy set; always popular due to the mascot factor. Limited Pikachu ex SIR is the key card.
  • Prismatic Evolutions (January 2025) — Eevee and Eeveelutions themed set; one of the most popular sets in years with stunning collector appeal and immediate sell-out at retail

The Chase Cards: SIRs Worth Owning

Special Illustration Rares are the defining collectibles of the SV era. Here are the most valuable and beloved SIRs across the format:

  • Charizard ex SIR (Obsidian Flames) — The undisputed king of SV-era collector cards; PSA 10 copies: $350–$600
  • Umbreon ex SIR (Prismatic Evolutions) — Stunning nighttime artwork; instant fan favorite. PSA 10: $180–$300
  • Vaporeon ex SIR (Prismatic Evolutions) — Among the most artistically acclaimed cards in the entire SV era. PSA 10: $150–$250
  • Pikachu ex SIR (Surging Sparks) — Pikachu cards always command premiums; SIR version with trainer Nemona is exceptional. PSA 10: $120–$200
  • Gardevoir ex SIR (Scarlet & Violet Base) — The original SV SIR that set collector expectations for the era. PSA 10: $100–$180
  • Miriam SIR (Scarlet & Violet Base) — Supporter card SIR; elegant artwork, strong demand from completionists. PSA 10: $80–$150
"The Special Illustration Rares of the SV era represent the most artistically ambitious Pokémon cards ever printed. In 20 years, collectors will look back at the Obsidian Flames Charizard ex SIR the way today's collectors view vintage Wizards holos — as defining artifacts of their era." — Sarah Rodriguez, ACT Collectibles

Should You Open Packs or Buy Singles?

The eternal Pokémon question. Here's the honest answer for SV sets:

  • Buy singles for investment — Opening packs is statistically negative EV for most buyers. The average hobby box does not contain enough SIRs to justify box prices against the singles market.
  • Open packs for the experience — If you enjoy the thrill of opening, blaster boxes at $20–$30 are the best value entry point. Accept that you're paying for entertainment, not investment.
  • Elite Trainer Boxes for casual collecting — Good middle ground; guaranteed full-art rares, organized storage, and a fun collecting experience without overpaying.

Grading Pokémon SV Cards

PSA, CGC, and BGS all actively grade SV cards. Key tips:

  • Pull cards carefully — SV card stock can show micro-scratches on the holo surface from pack contact
  • Focus grading budget on SIRs and key Hyper Rares where PSA 10 adds meaningful premium
  • CGC is increasingly competitive for Pokémon grading with faster turnarounds and lower costs for economy service
