Is Grading Worth It in 2026? The Real ROI Math for PSA, BGS, and CGC After the Price Hikes

TC
Tyler Chen
Grading & Market Editor · ACT Collectibles

PSA's February 2026 price hikes, stretched Bulk turnaround times now hitting 160 business days, and a changing competitive landscape from CGC have fundamentally shifted the grading ROI calculation. For most modern cards under $150 raw, selling ungraded is now the smarter financial move. For key rookies, vintage, and anything commanding a meaningful PSA 10 premium — the math still works. Here's the full 2026 breakdown.

The 2026 Cost Structure: All Three Companies

PSA currently operates three main tiers for individual collectors: Value Bulk at $24.99/card (Collector's Club members, 50-card minimum, up to 160 days), Value at $32.99/card (roughly 90 days), and faster premium tiers escalating to $300+ for next-day service. BGS base starts at $17.95/card with sub-grades. CGC has emerged as the speed-and-cost leader at under $20/card with 30–40 day returns across many service levels.

🔍
2026 Base Grading Costs:
PSA Value Bulk: $24.99/card (members, 50+ cards, up to 160 days)
PSA Value: $32.99/card (~90 days)
BGS Base: $17.95/card + sub-grades
CGC Standard: <$20/card · 30–40 day turnaround
SGC Economy: ~$18/card · 45–60 days

When PSA Still Wins

PSA commands the highest resale premiums on eBay across virtually every sports card category. For any card where a PSA 10 designation commands more than a 3x multiplier over raw value, the grade is worth pursuing at the appropriate tier. Key examples in 2026: Cooper Flagg Topps Chrome autos (PSA 10 commands 2.5x–4x raw), vintage baseball rookies from 1950s–1970s (PSA grades are essentially mandatory for maximum value), and any Pokémon card from Base Set through Neo where PSA 10 populations remain thin.

For high-value cards where you need the graded certification for buyer confidence, PSA's market recognition is still unmatched. A $500+ raw card going to PSA's Value tier at $32.99 represents a cost of approximately 6.6% of the card's value — that's a reasonable transaction fee if the PSA 10 multiplier pays out.

When CGC Wins

CGC has been closing the gap on PSA's market premium specifically in the Pokémon and One Piece TCG categories. Its 30–40 day turnaround and under-$20/card pricing make it the clear winner for modern Pokémon hauls where you want grades back within a single card season. For One Piece TCG specifically, CGC's market share within that community has grown to the point where CGC-graded copies of key cards trade at near-parity with PSA on several popular platforms.

CGC also wins for anyone building a registry collection in a category where PSA's registry isn't dominant. In comics, CGC owns the market entirely. Its card grading leverages that brand trust in adjacent collecting communities.

When BGS Still Makes Sense

Despite the Collectors Holdings acquisition, BGS retains a genuine technical advantage for the highest-precision condition evaluation. The sub-grade system — separate scores for corners, edges, surface, and centering — gives buyers and sellers more information than PSA or CGC provide. For modern cards where centering is the primary variable (common with factory-issued miscuts), a BGS 9.5 with pristine sub-grades tells a very different story than a plain "9.5."

The Bottom Line: When Not to Grade

Modern cards under $100 raw are almost never worth grading at current 2026 price points — unless you have a very high confidence the card grades PSA 10. At $32.99 per card for PSA Value and 90-day wait, you need a PSA 10 value of at least $100 just to break even, and that's before accounting for eBay fees, shipping, and capital tied up in the submission queue.

💡
2026 Grading Decision Tree: Card raw value $150+? Consider grading. PSA 10 multiplier 2x+? Send to PSA. Need it back fast (<45 days)? Use CGC. Card is Pokémon or One Piece? CGC or PSA both valid. High-value vintage? PSA mandatory for liquidity. Under $100 raw? Sell raw, skip the fees.
