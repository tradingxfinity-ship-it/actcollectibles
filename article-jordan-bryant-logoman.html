$12,932,000: The Michael Jordan & Kobe Bryant Dual Logoman Just Became the Most Expensive Sports Card Ever Sold at Public Auction

JM
James Mitchell
Senior Card Market Analyst · ACT Collectibles

On August 24, 2025, a single trading card changed the definition of what a sports collectible can be worth. A 2007-08 Upper Deck Exquisite Collection Dual Logoman — featuring game-worn logo patches and on-card autographs from both Michael Jordan and Kobe Bryant — sold for $12,932,000 at Heritage Auctions' Summer Platinum Night event in Dallas, Texas. It is now the most expensive sports card ever sold at a public auction, and it isn't close.

What Exactly Is a Logoman?

Before we talk about the price, it's worth understanding what makes a Logoman so rare. Upper Deck's Exquisite Collection series, produced in the mid-2000s, was the most premium NBA card product of its era. Each pack cost hundreds of dollars and contained a handful of ultra-limited cards. The crown jewel of any Exquisite product was the Logoman — a card in which the actual logo patch from a player's game-worn jersey (the circular NBA logo sewn onto every jersey) was cut out and embedded directly into a one-of-one card.

There is, by definition, only one logo patch per jersey. Which means there is only one Logoman card per player per set. The 2007-08 Dual Logoman took that concept even further: it contains both Jordan's and Bryant's logo patches on the same card, along with both players' autographs. There is no other card like it on earth. Period.

🏆
The Card: 2007-08 Upper Deck Exquisite Collection Dual Logoman — Jordan + Bryant
Grade: PSA 6 (EX-MT)
Sale Price: $12,932,000 (after 82 bids)
Venue: Heritage Auctions Summer Platinum Night, Dallas, TX
Previous Record: $12.6M — 1952 Topps Mickey Mantle PSA 9 (2022)

How the Auction Unfolded

Heritage Auctions entered the event with a pre-auction estimate of $6 million — already a staggering figure for a single collectible. The estimate proved to be dramatically conservative. Bidding opened strong and escalated through 82 rounds, with the hammer finally falling at $12,932,000. The final price more than doubled the pre-sale estimate and surpassed the previous all-time public record — $12.6 million paid for a 1952 Topps Mickey Mantle graded PSA 9 back in 2022 — by roughly $300,000.

What makes the result even more remarkable is the card's grade. The Mantle that set the previous record was a PSA 9 — near-pristine. The Jordan-Bryant Logoman is graded PSA 6, a mid-grade that reflects some wear to the card's corners and surface. In most categories of collecting, a PSA 6 sells at a significant discount to higher grades. Here, condition was essentially irrelevant: the content of the card — the physical pieces of game-worn fabric from two of the greatest players ever, authenticated and encased forever — is what commands the price.

This card had sold before. Its prior sale price was $3.17 million. In other words, it more than quadrupled in value between transactions, a staggering appreciation that underscores how rapidly the top tier of the sports card market has moved over the past five years.

Legacy, Not Just Numbers

Both players need no introduction, but the timing of this sale carries particular weight. Kobe Bryant passed away in January 2020. Since then, his collectibles market has surged consistently, as collectors and fans recognize there will never be new Kobe content, new Kobe autographs, or new Kobe game-worn material entering the hobby. His cards are, in the truest sense, finite. Michael Jordan's market remains the strongest in basketball at any grade level, buoyed by the ongoing fascination with the Jordan era driven in part by The Last Dance documentary. Together, the two represent the apex of 1990s and 2000s NBA basketball — and this card is the single most concentrated expression of both legacies in cardboard form.

What This Means for the Hobby

Records like this have a ripple effect. When the Mantle sold for $12.6M in 2022, demand for high-grade vintage baseball cards spiked across the board. Expect a similar dynamic here: 1-of-1 Logomans of Jordan and Bryant individually, dual-player patch cards, and high-grade Exquisite Collection autos will all see renewed attention from both buyers and sellers in the months ahead.

Heritage Auctions itself reported closing 2025 above $2.15 billion in total sales — a new benchmark for the company. The sports card category has been a major driver of that growth. Other notable 2025 results included a Michael Jordan/LeBron James Dual Logoman (2006-07 Exquisite, also a 1/1) that sold for approximately $10 million in a private sale, and a Shohei Ohtani 2025 Topps Chrome Gold Logoman 1/1 that achieved $3 million.

The lesson for collectors: the very top of the market — true 1-of-1 items combining the biggest names in sports history with authentic game-used material — operates in a different universe from everything else. Supply is permanently zero. Demand, fueled by a global collector base and expanding wealth among sports fans, is only growing. The $12.93 million Jordan-Bryant Logoman may be a record today. It may not be one for long.

💡
Key Takeaway for Collectors: 1-of-1 game-used cards from all-time legends are the hardest possible asset in the hobby. Unlike parallel cards with print runs in the hundreds, there is no possible supply increase. If you own one, you own the only one. That scarcity, combined with the global growth of sports card collecting, is what drives prices like this.
