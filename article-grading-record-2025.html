Trading Card Grading Hits Record Numbers in 2025 — What the Data Tells Us

DL
David Lee
Senior Grading Analyst
·August 12, 2025·8 min read·✓ Expert Verified

The trading card grading industry is showing no signs of slowing down. According to data compiled across PSA, BGS, SGC, and CGC, more than 5 million cards were submitted for grading in the first six months of 2025 alone — a pace that puts the industry on track for another all-time annual record.

What's driving this sustained boom, and what does it mean for collectors navigating the market today? We dig into the numbers.

The Grading Boom by the Numbers

Grading volume exploded during the COVID-era hobby surge of 2020–2021, and while many expected a sharp correction, submissions have remained consistently high. Here's how 2025 compares to recent years:

📊 Annual Grading Submissions (Estimated, All Companies)

  • 2020: ~4.2 million cards graded — the pandemic boom begins
  • 2021: ~9.5 million cards — all-time peak, PSA temporarily closes submissions
  • 2022: ~9.5 million cards — sustained record pace
  • 2023: ~8.8 million cards — slight dip as market normalizes
  • 2024: ~9.1 million cards — graders enter year on record pace
  • 2025 (H1 only): ~5.1 million cards — on pace for another record year

CGC Cards: The Fastest-Growing Grader

While PSA remains the dominant name in sports card grading, CGC Cards — the trading card arm of the well-established comic book grader CGC — has emerged as the fastest-growing competitor in the space. Their share of monthly submissions has grown substantially year-over-year, particularly in the Pokémon and tabletop TCG categories.

CGC's clean label aesthetic, competitive pricing, and strong reputation in the collectibles world have won over a significant segment of TCG collectors who previously defaulted to PSA or BGS.

What's Being Graded in 2025?

The composition of what collectors are submitting has shifted meaningfully since 2021:

  • Modern sports rookies — Still the backbone of PSA submissions; Cooper Flagg and the 2025 NBA draft class dominate new volumes
  • Pokémon TCG — Consistently accounting for a large share of PSA and CGC volume, driven by ongoing nostalgia demand for Base Set and newer chase sets
  • Vintage baseball — PSA's most-graded vintage card remains the 1980 Topps Rickey Henderson RC, with over 41,000 copies now encapsulated
  • WWE and non-sport cards — A growing category following Topps' new WWE licensing deal, with collectors rushing to grade early Hulk Hogan and Stone Cold cards

PSA's Most Graded Cards of 2024

PSA's annual data for 2024 revealed some notable trends in what vintage baseball collectors are prioritizing:

  • The 1975 Topps George Brett RC (#228) saw approximately 2,000 newly graded copies last year, bringing total PSA population to over 17,600
  • The 1980 Topps Rickey Henderson RC remains the all-time most-graded vintage card with over 41,000 PSA-encapsulated copies
  • The 1989 Upper Deck Ken Griffey Jr. Star Rookie (#1) leads the modern era with about 10,100 graded copies — only 4% receiving a PSA 10

Turnaround Times: Faster Than Ever

One of the most collector-friendly developments of 2025 is the dramatic improvement in PSA turnaround times. After notorious delays that stretched to over a year during the 2021 peak, PSA is now processing approximately 26,000+ cards per day, bringing economy service turnarounds down to 60–90 days.

BGS and SGC have similarly improved, making grading more accessible to the average collector rather than just professional flippers who could afford expedited service.

"The normalization of grading turnaround times is one of the best things to happen to the hobby in years. It means collectors can actually grade cards in a reasonable timeframe and make investment decisions based on real market conditions." — David Lee, ACT Collectibles

What This Means for Collectors

The sustained grading volume tells us a few important things about the state of the hobby:

  • The hobby is healthy — Grading submissions are a reliable proxy for overall collector engagement
  • Population creep is real — As more cards get graded, PSA 10 populations rise, which can suppress prices for common cards over time
  • Authentication matters more than ever — With the market this active, counterfeit and altered cards are a real risk; grading provides critical protection
  • Competition is good for consumers — CGC and SGC challenging PSA's dominance keeps prices competitive and innovation moving

Should You Still Grade Your Cards in 2025?

Absolutely — but be selective. With high grading volumes driving up PSA populations on common cards, focus your grading budget on:

  • Cards where PSA 10 still commands a 3x–10x premium over raw
  • Hot rookies in their first season (submit quickly before the market softens)
  • Vintage cards where authentication adds real credibility and value
  • Low-pop cards where your submission could be a significant percentage of the PSA 10 population
