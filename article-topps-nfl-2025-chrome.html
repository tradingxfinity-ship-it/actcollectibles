Topps Is Back in the NFL: Everything Collectors Need to Know About 2025 Topps Chrome Football

JM
James Mitchell
Senior Card Market Analyst · ACT Collectibles

It's been nine years. Nine years since a Topps logo appeared on a licensed NFL trading card. That drought ended on April 15, 2025, when Fanatics Collectibles — the company that acquired Topps in 2022 — released 2025 Topps Chrome Football, the first fully licensed Topps NFL product since 2016. For collectors who grew up with Topps football, this isn't just a product launch. It's the return of an era.

How We Got Here: The License War That Reshaped the Hobby

In 2016, Panini outbid Topps for the exclusive NFL trading card license, ending a relationship between Topps and football that stretched back decades. For nine seasons, Panini owned NFL cardboard entirely — producing Prizm, Select, Mosaic, Donruss, and dozens of other sets that defined an entire generation of football collecting.

Then Fanatics entered the picture. The e-commerce giant had been quietly positioning itself to dominate licensed sports merchandise, and in 2022 it acquired Topps — along with the MLB license that came with it. From there, the strategy accelerated. Fanatics secured a new NBA license deal in 2025 (ending Panini's basketball run) and then signed a multi-year agreement with the NFL and the NFLPA to become the league's exclusive trading card partner beginning April 2026 — with a product launch in April 2025 to signal the transition.

Panini, for its part, has not gone quietly. The Italian company filed an antitrust lawsuit against Fanatics alleging monopolization of the licensed sports card market. That litigation is ongoing, adding a layer of legal uncertainty to the 2026 transition period. But for 2025, the product is real, the license is signed, and Topps Chrome Football is on store shelves.

What's in the Box: Key Products and Chase Cards

Chrome has always been Topps' signature technology — that distinctive refractor shine that catches light from every angle. Applied to NFL rookies for the first time in nearly a decade, the results are stunning. Here's what to target:

🔥
Top Chase Cards in 2025 Topps Chrome Football
Rookie PREM1ERE Patch Autos (1/1), Gold NFL Shield Patch Autos, Superfractors (1/1), Ultra Violet parallels, and retail-exclusive Fanatical case hits — all new to football.

Rookie PREM1ERE Patch Autographs (1/1)

This is the product's marquee insert and it's genuinely unprecedented in NFL collecting. These 1-of-1 cards contain patches physically removed from jerseys worn by rookies during their actual NFL debut game — authenticated by Topps on-site and embedded directly into the card. This means the card you pull can never be replicated. Ever. Once a player's debut is over, that's the only game-worn patch from that game that will ever exist in a card. 2025 subjects include Jaxson Dart, Cam Skattebo, Cam Ward, and TreVeyon Henderson.

Gold NFL Shield Patch Autographs (1/1)

The gold NFL shield patch — the circular logo sewn into the collar of every official NFL jersey — is the holy grail of game-used memorabilia for any set. Topps is producing these 1-of-1 cards for the league's biggest stars from the 2024-25 season. Confirmed subjects include Josh Allen (MVP), Saquon Barkley (Offensive Player of the Year), Jayden Daniels (Offensive Rookie of the Year), Pat Surtain II (Defensive Player of the Year), and Jared Verse (Defensive Rookie of the Year). Pull one of these from a hobby box and you've won the lottery.

Refractor Parallels

The classic Chrome rainbow returns for football. Base Refractors, Blue, Green, Gold (/50), Orange (/25), Red (/5), and Superfractor (1/1) — the structure veteran Chrome collectors know well, now applied to a full NFL rookie class for the first time since 2016.

New Inserts: Ultra Violet and Fanatical

Topps is introducing two new insert lines exclusive to football: Ultra Violet parallels appearing in hobby boxes and Fanatical case hits available at retail. Both are making their football debut, giving the set a distinct identity separate from Topps' baseball and basketball products.

The 2025 Rookie Class: Who to Target

The quality of a Chrome product lives or dies with its rookie class. The 2025 NFL Draft gave Topps an exceptional group to work with:

Cam Ward (QB, Tennessee Titans) — The consensus top quarterback of the class, selected first overall. Ward's early arc mirrors the kind of trajectory that makes rookie cards valuable: a confident, established starter with immediate expectations on a rebuilding franchise that needs him to be the guy. His Chrome auto is the most sought-after of the class at launch.

Travis Hunter (WR/CB, Cleveland Browns) — The Heisman winner and the most unique prospect in years, Hunter plays both sides of the ball at the NFL level. The dual-position novelty creates natural collector interest: every future Hunter card could be a receiver card, a defensive back card, or both.

Ashton Jeanty (RB, Las Vegas Raiders) — Running backs don't generate the collector interest they once did, but Jeanty is the exception. His college production was historic and the Raiders' offensive system is designed around him. A strong start pushes his early Chrome cards significantly.

Jaxson Dart (QB, New York Giants) — Selected in the first round and handed the keys to a storied franchise, Dart benefits from the Giants' massive market and fan base. New York quarterbacks who hit have outsized card value. His PREM1ERE Patch Auto is the most coveted of all the debut cards.

Where to Buy

2025 Topps Chrome Football is available at Topps.com, hobby shops nationwide, NFLShop.com, and Fanatics Live. Hobby boxes retail around $180–$220; blaster boxes for retail collectors run $30–$40. Given the 9-year absence and the first-edition collector premium, expect hobby boxes to trade significantly above retail on the secondary market through at least mid-2025.

💡
Collector's Take: The "first Topps NFL product in 9 years" narrative is real and it matters. First editions of historic products — the first Topps football card of a new era — carry a storytelling premium that persists for decades. Buy what you love, but don't underestimate what these early cards mean historically.
