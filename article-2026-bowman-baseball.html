2026 Bowman Baseball Is Here: Ethan Holliday Headlines a Deep Prospect Set With New Anime Cards and WBC Parallels

JM
James Mitchell
Sports Card Editor · ACT Collectibles

Bowman Baseball is the most important annual release in the hobby for prospect collectors — and 2026 doesn't disappoint. Released May 13, the set features Colorado Rockies top prospect Ethan Holliday as its centerpiece, a deep 150-card Chrome Prospect set, returning favorites like Bowman Spotlights, and a new manga-inspired Anime series that includes World Baseball Classic cards for the first time. Hobby boxes hit the Topps site at $239.99 on presale; they're now $259.99 at retail.

The Featured Prospect: Ethan Holliday

Ethan Holliday — son of MLB legend Matt Holliday — was the No. 4 overall pick in the 2025 MLB Draft and currently sits as the Colorado Rockies' top-ranked prospect. Bowman chose him as the face of the 2026 set, meaning his First Bowman Card (the first officially licensed Bowman card for a prospect) is the most-hunted autograph in the set. His Chrome Prospect Autograph in the base refractor is already trading in the $180–$250 range raw; graded PSA 10 examples are moving closer to $400.

Holliday is a shortstop with elite bat-to-ball skills and strong arm strength. His upside comp is a .290 hitter with 25+ home run potential — the kind of profile that sustains card value even if he takes a few years to reach the majors.

Key Inserts and New Products

The Bowman Red Rookie Card variations return for their second year — short-printed cards with a bold red background that have become instant chase items. Last year's RCs sold out within hours of release and have held strong secondary market value. The Bowman Spotlight insert features high-contrast light-blast photography and returns as one of the set's most visually striking inserts.

The real headline addition is the Anime series — manga-inspired full-art designs with World Baseball Classic cards of Team USA and Team Japan players. The rarest pull in this subset: Kanji Variations of Japanese players, numbered to 25 or less. These have the design aesthetic of a collectible that plays well beyond the traditional baseball card audience, and early secondary market results suggest collectors are paying attention.

The new Chrome Prospect PackFractor Variation (/89) takes 1989 Bowman Baseball packaging art and translates it into a Chrome refractor format — a perfect nostalgia play that serious set collectors will want alongside the modern prospect chase.

What to Chase and What to Skip

The smartest targets in 2026 Bowman remain on-card autographs of the top 5 prospect rankings. Holliday leads, but watch for first Bowman cards of anyone in Baseball America's top 50. Mid-tier prospects from large-market teams tend to carry a hometown premium. Avoid parallels numbered above /150 for investment purposes — the market concentrates value in /50 and below.

The Topps Value Blasters at $29.99 are pure pack fun — don't expect investment-grade pulls. For serious collectors, the Hobby box (one auto) or Jumbo (three autos) configurations are the only paths to the autographs that actually carry meaningful secondary value.

💡
Best Targets: Ethan Holliday Chrome Prospect Auto · Any top-50 prospect First Bowman Chrome · Anime Kanji Variations /25 or less · Bowman Spotlight SPs. Skip: high-numbered parallels, retail Blaster autos (lower-end prospects).
