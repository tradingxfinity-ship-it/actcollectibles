2024-25 NBA Panini Prizm: Best Rookies, Key Cards & Why the Final Panini Era Matters

JM
James Mitchell
Senior Sports Card Analyst
·January 22, 2025·8 min read·✓ Expert Verified

The 2024-25 Panini Prizm NBA set carries a weight that no previous Prizm release has had: it is almost certainly the last NBA Prizm set Panini will ever produce. With Fanatics/Topps taking over the NBA license after this season, every Silver Prizm, Gold Prizm, and numbered parallel in this product is officially part of the final chapter of a remarkable run that began in 2012-13.

That historical context — combined with a genuinely exciting rookie class — makes the 2024-25 set one of the most collector-significant Prizm releases in years.

The "Last Panini" Premium

Throughout hobby history, "final year" products from departing license holders have carried long-term collector premiums. The 2009-10 Topps NBA set — the last before Panini took over — is now viewed as historically significant and commands premiums for key cards that comparable-year Panini products don't.

The same dynamic is now playing out in reverse. Collectors who understand this pattern are quietly accumulating PSA 10 Silver Prizm rookies from the 2024-25 set, betting that the "final Panini Prizm NBA" narrative adds durable value over time.

Top Rookies in the 2024-25 Prizm NBA Set

⭐ Best Rookie Cards to Target — 2024-25 Panini Prizm NBA

  • Zaccharie Risacher (Atlanta Hawks, #1 Pick) — The French forward's Prizm RC has steady demand; watch his development closely for price trajectory
  • Alexandre Sarr (Washington Wizards, #2 Pick) — High-upside big man; his RC is undervalued relative to his draft position if he develops as scouts project
  • Reed Sheppard (Houston Rockets, #3 Pick) — Elite shooter; Silver Prizm PSA 10 is the target card for his collectors
  • Rob Dillingham (Minnesota Timberwolves, #8 Pick) — Electrifying scorer; early-season performances have driven RC prices upward
  • Stephon Castle (San Antonio Spurs, #4 Pick) — Playing alongside Victor Wembanyama; market interest is strong based on teammate proximity
  • Dalton Knecht (Los Angeles Lakers, #17 Pick) — Lakers tax applies; any Laker RC has inflated collector demand regardless of draft slot

Established Stars Worth Chasing in Prizm 2024-25

Beyond rookies, the 2024-25 Prizm set features key cards of established superstars that carry perennial collector demand:

  • Victor Wembanyama — His second-year Prizm cards are must-haves; Gold Prizm /10 copies are the prestige play
  • Chet Holmgren — Third-year card; established his NBA legitimacy with a strong sophomore season
  • Luka Dončić — Now with the Lakers; any new Luka card in purple and gold will carry a market premium
  • Nikola Jokić — Three-time MVP; his cards are systematically undervalued relative to his historical greatness
  • SGA (Shai Gilgeous-Alexander) — MVP-level performer; Silver Prizm PSA 10s are strong long-term holds

Parallel Guide: Which Prizm Colors to Target

The Prizm parallel rainbow is the defining feature of the product. Here's how the key parallels rank by value and collectability:

  • Silver Prizm (base parallel) — The most collected and traded parallel; PSA 10 of top rookies is the volume-driver card
  • Green Prizm /149 — Affordable numbered entry with solid demand
  • Blue Ice Prizm /199 — Popular retail parallel; visually striking
  • Red Prizm /299 — Common but perpetually in demand for popular players
  • Gold Prizm /10 — The most desirable non-1/1 parallel; price can be 10–50x the Silver on hot players
  • Black Prizm 1/1 — Trophy card; any Black 1/1 of a star player commands serious money
  • Rookie Autographs — On-card autos of top picks; the best value play in the product for high-ceiling prospects
"Collecting the final Panini Prizm NBA set is like owning a piece of an era. Collectors in 2035 will look back at the 2024-25 Prizm the way today's collectors look at 2009-10 Topps NBA — as the end of a chapter worth remembering." — James Mitchell, ACT Collectibles

Where to Find 2024-25 Prizm NBA

  • Hobby boxes — Available at local card shops; includes guaranteed autographs and more numbered parallels per box
  • Mega/blaster boxes — Retail versions at Target and Walmart; more accessible price point, good for building the base set
  • eBay and COMC — Best for targeting specific rookies and high-grade singles without opening packs
  • Card shows — Dealers carry Prizm inventory; negotiation possible on bulk purchases
