2025 Prizm WNBA: Paige Bueckers, Caitlin Clark & the Cards Reshaping Women's Basketball Collecting

SR
Sarah Rodriguez
TCG & Women's Sports Cards Editor
·July 18, 2025·8 min read·✓ Expert Verified

Women's basketball card collecting has never been hotter. The arrival of Paige Bueckers as the #1 overall WNBA Draft pick — joining an already electric league featuring Caitlin Clark, A'ja Wilson, and Angel Reese — has sent the 2025 Prizm WNBA set soaring in collector demand before boxes even hit shelves.

If you haven't been paying attention to WNBA cards, now is the time to start.

Why WNBA Cards Are Surging Right Now

Three years ago, WNBA cards were a niche within a niche. That changed dramatically when Caitlin Clark entered the league in 2024. Her 2024 Prizm WNBA rookie cards — available at Target in blaster boxes — became some of the most sought-after retail items in the country, with stores selling out within hours of restocking.

The 2025 class amplifies that trend. Paige Bueckers brings one of the most recognizable names in college basketball history to the Connecticut Sun, giving the WNBA another marquee face whose cards collectors are desperate to own.

Key Players to Collect in 2025 Prizm WNBA

⭐ Top Cards to Target in 2025 Prizm WNBA

  • Paige Bueckers RC Prizm Base — The #1 rookie in the class; expect PSA 10 copies to command $150–$400 at launch, rising sharply if she wins Rookie of the Year
  • Paige Bueckers Silver Prizm RC /199 — The key numbered parallel; strong demand from both basketball and Connecticut fans
  • Caitlin Clark Prizm Base — Her second-year card; the market for Clark remains red-hot as she develops into a WNBA superstar
  • A'ja Wilson Prizm — The two-time MVP and face of the Las Vegas Aces; her cards have steady, long-term collector demand
  • Angel Reese RC (2024, now Year 2) — Chicago Sky star whose popularity off the court drives strong card demand
  • Kiki Iriafen RC — Dark-horse rookie pick; high upside if she earns significant playing time early
  • Breanna Stewart Prizm — One of the most decorated WNBA players ever; her cards are undervalued relative to her career achievements

Prizm WNBA Parallel Rainbow — What to Know

The 2025 Prizm WNBA set follows the same parallel structure collectors know from the NBA and NFL versions. Here's what to look for:

  • Base Prizm — The standard silver refractor; most accessible entry point
  • Silver Prizm /199 — First numbered parallel; strong trade and resale value
  • Gold Prizm /10 — Extremely limited; any Bueckers or Clark copy at this number will sell for significant money
  • Black Prizm 1/1 — The rarest of the rainbow; a true trophy card
  • Hyper Prizm — The retail-exclusive colorful parallel; wildly popular with younger collectors
  • Rookie Autographs — On-card autos are available at hobby box rates; the Bueckers auto is the chase card of the entire set

Where WNBA Card Values Are Heading

The trajectory for top WNBA cards is clearly upward, driven by several converging factors:

  • Growing broadcast deals — The WNBA's new media rights agreement brings more eyeballs to the game, expanding the potential collector base
  • Star power — The current generation of WNBA players has crossover cultural appeal that previous eras didn't
  • Underpriced relative to NBA — A Caitlin Clark PSA 10 rookie still sells for dramatically less than a comparable male basketball star; that gap will likely narrow
  • Female collector community growth — One of the fastest-growing demographics in the hobby; WNBA cards resonate deeply with this audience
"A Paige Bueckers PSA 10 Silver Prizm rookie bought today at $300 could look like the bargain of the decade in five years — the same way Clark's earliest cards already do versus their 2024 launch prices." — Sarah Rodriguez, ACT Collectibles

How to Buy 2025 Prizm WNBA

  • Hobby boxes — Available at local card shops; typically include guaranteed autographs and more numbered parallels
  • Blaster boxes at Target/Walmart — More affordable entry; great for building the base set or pulling Hyper Prizms
  • eBay singles — The fastest way to target specific player cards without opening packs
  • Card shows — WNBA cards are increasingly showing up in dealer inventories at regional shows
