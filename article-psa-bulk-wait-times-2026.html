PSA Bulk Grading: Wait Times Now Hit 160 Business Days and the Minimum Jumps to 50 Cards

TC
Tyler Chen
Grading & Market Editor · ACT Collectibles

PSA just quietly dropped two changes that will reshape how everyday collectors approach bulk submissions. Effective May 18, 2026, the company's cheapest tier — Bulk Grading — now carries a maximum turnaround window of 140 to 160 business days, up from the previous 95-day ceiling. On top of that, the minimum card count per submission has jumped from 20 to 50 cards. If you were planning a small modern haul, you'll need to rethink your strategy.

What Exactly Changed

PSA's Bulk tier is available exclusively to paid annual PSA Collector's Club members. It's the most affordable way to get cards graded at scale — historically it's been where the bulk of modern Pokémon, sports, and TCG submissions land. But as PSA continues to process record submission volumes (up 39% year-over-year in 2026), the company is clearly trying to manage the queue.

The two specific changes are: first, the maximum turnaround estimate extends from 95 to between 140 and 160 business days. A submission sent in today could realistically not be returned until January 2027. Second, the per-submission minimum card count rises from 20 to 50. This eliminates the small-batch submission strategy many collectors relied on to test grading ROI before committing to a larger haul.

📋
Old Bulk Rules: 20-card minimum · up to 95 business days
New Bulk Rules (May 18, 2026): 50-card minimum · up to 160 business days
Tier Price: $24.99/card (Collector's Club members only)
Who's Affected: Anyone submitting modern cards at volume

Why Is PSA Doing This?

Volume. PSA now grades approximately 90,000 cards per day globally. In 2020 they graded 2 million cards for the full year — in 2025 alone they processed more than 19 million. A 39% increase year-over-year going into 2026 means PSA's facilities are straining under the load even amid a reported $200 million facility expansion. Lengthening turnaround times on the lowest-priced tier is a classic demand-management tool: it discourages low-value submissions without cutting off the tier entirely.

The 50-card minimum serves a similar function. Small submitters — particularly those testing a new card or doing a speculative one-off — are essentially being priced out of Bulk. The economics now favor collectors who are already committed to grading at scale.

What This Means for Your Grading Strategy

If you grade modern cards for resale, the math has shifted. With the Value Bulk tier sitting at $24.99/card and wait times potentially running to January 2027, you're tying up capital for an extended period. A card you submit today might not be back in your hands for over eight months. Factor in carrying cost, market movement risk (cards can drop in value while they sit in the grading queue), and the 50-card minimum commitment, and the calculation starts to look different from six months ago.

Faster tiers remain available at higher price points. PSA's Value tier is $32.99/card with a roughly 90-day turnaround. CGC — which has been gaining market share — offers standard grading under $20/card with 30–40 day returns in many cases. For Pokémon collectors specifically, CGC has been closing the gap on PSA's market premium, making it a legitimate alternative for modern sets.

The Bigger Picture

These changes don't exist in a vacuum. PSA's parent company Collectors Holdings is in the process of acquiring Beckett (BGS), pending any regulatory action. If that deal closes, Collectors Holdings will control PSA, SGC, and BGS — roughly 79% of the grading market. Critics argue that reduced competition may allow PSA to push prices and wait times upward without collectors having strong alternatives. For now, CGC and SGC remain independent competitive options.

💡
Action Plan: If you're planning a Bulk submission, batch up to at least 50 cards to meet the new minimum. Consider CGC for sub-100-card modern hauls where turnaround matters. For high-value vintage, the PSA premium is still worth faster tiers. Run your cost basis math before submitting — at 160 days, market conditions will have shifted.
