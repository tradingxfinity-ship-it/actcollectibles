Topps Signs Long-Term WWE Trading Card Deal — What Collectors Need to Know

MP
Maria Perez
Non-Sport & Entertainment Cards Editor
·January 8, 2025·6 min read·✓ Expert Verified

Professional wrestling fans and card collectors have long memories — and many of them remember the classic Topps WWE sets of the 1980s and early 1990s fondly. Now, Topps has announced a new long-term licensing deal with WWE to produce trading cards, bringing the iconic brand back to the wrestling market in a major way starting in 2025.

This isn't just a one-off release. Topps and WWE have committed to a multi-year partnership, meaning collectors can expect consistent annual releases, autograph programs, and premium products for the foreseeable future.

A Brief History of WWE Trading Cards

Wrestling cards have a rich collecting tradition in America. The original Topps WWF sets from the mid-1980s — featuring Hulk Hogan, Randy "Macho Man" Savage, and Andre the Giant — are among the most nostalgic non-sport cards in the hobby. Panini held the WWE license for several years before losing it, leaving a gap in officially licensed wrestling card production.

Topps' return fills that void and carries enormous weight given their history with the brand.

What the New Topps WWE Sets Look Like

The first wave of new Topps WWE products includes several distinct tiers:

🎯 2025 Topps WWE Product Lineup

  • Topps WWE Base Set — 200-card flagship set covering all major current stars, legends, and hall of famers; hobby and retail versions available
  • Topps WWE Chrome — Chromium parallel technology applied to wrestling cards; expect Refractor and Prizm-style parallels
  • Topps WWE Finest — Premium autograph product; on-card autos from current superstars and legends
  • Topps WWE Legends — A dedicated legends-only product covering the full history of WWE, with vintage photo autos from icons like Ric Flair, "Stone Cold" Steve Austin, and The Rock
  • Topps WWE Slam Attax — The popular casual/game card series returns; great entry point for younger collectors

Stars and Legends to Collect

The new Topps WWE lineup covers both the current roster and decades of legends. Standout names to target:

  • Cody Rhodes — Current WWE Champion; his cards are the hottest in modern WWE collecting
  • Roman Reigns — The "Tribal Chief" era produced some of the most creative autograph cards in recent memory
  • Becky Lynch, Rhea Ripley, Bianca Belair — Women's division superstars with strong collector bases
  • Hulk Hogan, "Stone Cold" Steve Austin, The Rock — Legend autos command significant premiums; low print runs make them highly desirable
  • André the Giant — Vintage authenticated signatures are among the most valuable wrestling collectibles in existence

Vintage WWE Cards to Watch Right Now

The new Topps deal has renewed interest in vintage wrestling cards, and collectors are rushing to grade their old packs. Here's what's surging in value:

  • 1985 Topps WWF Wrestling Stars — The Hulk Hogan card (#1) in PSA 9 or better is a grail piece; raw copies sell for $20–$80, PSA 9 copies for $400+
  • 1990 Classic WWF cards — The Ultimate Warrior and Bret Hart cards from this set are heating up
  • 1996 Cardz WCW/NWO sets — The Monday Night Wars era cards are criminally undervalued; early nWo cards featuring Scott Hall and Kevin Nash are worth a look
  • 2002 Fleer WWE Raw vs. SmackDown — Early John Cena and Brock Lesnar appearances; Cena's rookie-era cards have tripled in value over three years
"Wrestling card collecting has always been a niche within a niche — but with Topps back on the license and WWE as culturally dominant as ever, this category is about to go mainstream. Get in before the casual collectors show up." — Maria Perez, ACT Collectibles

Should You Collect WWE Cards?

For collectors looking for a less saturated market with strong upside, wrestling cards represent a compelling opportunity. The combination of nostalgia, an active current roster, and the backing of the Topps brand creates a favorable environment for both casual collecting and strategic investment.

The Topps WWE deal is long-term, which means sustained product pipelines, growing collector community awareness, and more opportunities to find value. Start with a classic vintage piece, add a Cody Rhodes auto from the new Topps set, and you've got a balanced WWE card portfolio.
