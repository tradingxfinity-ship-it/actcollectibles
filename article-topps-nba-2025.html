Topps Returns to the NBA: First Licensed Basketball Cards Since 2009

JM
James Mitchell
Senior Sports Card Analyst
· October 23, 2025 · 9 min read · ✓ Expert Verified

For the first time since the 2009–10 season, Topps is officially back in the NBA trading card market — and collectors everywhere are paying close attention. The 2025–26 Topps Basketball set launched on October 23, 2025, marking the culmination of Fanatics Collectibles' massive licensing overhaul of the hobby.

After Panini held the exclusive NBA license for over a decade, Fanatics (which now owns Topps) stepped in and changed the game. The return of the iconic Topps brand to basketball is one of the most significant moments in modern card collecting history.

Why This Set Matters

Topps and basketball cards have deep historical roots. Before Panini took over in 2009, Topps produced some of the most beloved basketball sets ever made — including iconic releases featuring Kobe Bryant, Shaquille O'Neal, and Kevin Garnett. For older collectors, this return feels like a homecoming.

But this isn't nostalgia for nostalgia's sake. Fanatics has invested heavily in making the 2025–26 set a flagship product with modern design sensibilities, premium autograph content, and exclusive athlete deals that no other publisher can match.

Cooper Flagg: The Face of the Set

The undisputed star of the 2025–26 Topps Basketball release is Cooper Flagg, the #1 overall NBA Draft pick and Duke product who was selected by the Dallas Mavericks. Flagg is featured prominently across multiple insert sets and autograph tiers, and his rookie cards have already generated enormous pre-release demand.

🔥 Key Cooper Flagg Cards to Watch

  • Topps Base Rookie #101 — The flagship RC; expect PSA 10 copies to command a premium
  • Topps Chrome Refractor RC — The chromium parallel collectors have been waiting for
  • Topps Rookie Autograph /99 — Limited on-card auto; biggest value play in the set
  • Gold Refractor /50 — Parallel rainbow chasing opportunities
  • Superfractor 1/1 — The holy grail of the release

Set Format & Product Tiers

Like Topps sets of old, the 2025–26 release launches across multiple product tiers:

  • Topps Series 1 Basketball — The base product; hobby and retail versions available at Target, Walmart, and hobby shops nationwide
  • Topps Chrome Basketball — The premium chromium release; expected late 2025 / early 2026
  • Topps Finest Basketball — Ultra-premium product with on-card autographs and limited print runs
  • Topps Stadium Club Basketball — Photography-focused premium set for collectors who love image quality
"Topps is back, and they've come back swinging. Cooper Flagg rookie cards are already the most anticipated debut since Zion Williamson in 2019. If Flagg develops into the player scouts expect, his Topps RCs could be multi-thousand-dollar investments within five years." — James Mitchell, ACT Collectibles

Other Top Rookies in the 2025–26 Class

While Flagg is clearly the headliner, the 2025 NBA Draft class is genuinely deep. Collectors should also pay attention to:

  • Dylan Harper (New Jersey Nets, #2 pick) — Guard from Rutgers with elite scoring ability
  • Ace Bailey (Sacramento Kings, #3 pick) — Athletic wing with high upside
  • VJ Edgecombe (Philadelphia 76ers, #5 pick) — International guard generating buzz
  • Tre Johnson (Utah Jazz, #6 pick) — Pure scorer from Texas with a high ceiling

Where to Buy 2025–26 Topps Basketball

The set is available through multiple channels in the United States:

  • Fanatics.com — The official source; exclusive bundles available
  • Local hobby shops — Support your LCS for hobby boxes and breakers
  • Target & Walmart — Blaster boxes and hanger packs for retail versions
  • eBay — For singles of key rookies and autographs
  • COMC / Beckett Marketplace — For graded singles and portfolio-building

Our Verdict

The 2025–26 Topps Basketball set represents a genuine watershed moment for the hobby. Whether you're a long-time collector who remembers Topps basketball fondly, or a newer investor eyeing the Cooper Flagg rookie wave, this product deserves your attention.

For the first time in 16 years, Topps is on your basketball cards. That alone makes this a set worth collecting.
