Best NFL Rookie Cards to Buy Right Now — 2025 Draft Class Investor's Guide

JM
James Mitchell
Senior Sports Card Analyst
·May 2, 2025·8 min read·✓ Expert Verified

The 2025 NFL Draft class arrives at a historically unique moment: these are the first NFL rookie cards produced by Fanatics/Topps under their new exclusive license. Every quarterback, wide receiver, and defensive star drafted in 2025 will have their first official NFL rookie card bearing the Topps name — making this the most historically significant NFL RC class since the licensing era began.

Beyond the historic context, the draft class itself is genuinely deep. Here's who to buy, what to pay, and how to think about each player from a card investment perspective.

Why Landing Spot Matters for RC Values

In football cards more than any other sport, landing spot dramatically impacts rookie card value. Key factors:

  • Quarterback market size — QBs in large media markets (Dallas, New York, Los Angeles) carry market-tax premiums regardless of talent
  • Offensive system — Pass-heavy offenses produce more statistics; skill players in spread systems generate better fantasy relevance, which drives card interest
  • Starting opportunity — Immediate starters generate faster card appreciation; players in depth charts get delayed price movement
  • Team playoff trajectory — Players on contenders get more national exposure; playoff performances spike card prices dramatically

Top 2025 NFL Rookie Cards to Buy Now

🏈 2025 NFL Draft Class — Rookie Card Investment Ratings

  • Cam Ward (QB — Tennessee Titans, #1 Overall) — The consensus top QB in the class; Titans are rebuilding around him. First overall QBs almost always have strong RC markets regardless of team. Rating: Strong Buy on PSA 10 Topps Chrome RC
  • Travis Hunter (CB/WR — Jacksonville Jaguars, #2 Overall) — The most unique prospect in years: a two-way player who could generate points as both a receiver and defensive back. Dual-position novelty creates exceptional collector demand. Rating: Strong Buy — unique player narrative drives long-term interest
  • Ashton Jeanty (RB — Las Vegas Raiders, #6 Overall) — Boise State's record-breaking rusher. Running backs are historically undervalued in card markets but Jeanty's elite production and story make him compelling. Rating: Moderate Buy — RBs carry position risk but the talent is elite
  • Tetairoa McMillan (WR — Carolina Panthers, #8 Overall) — The top wide receiver in the class; massive frame, excellent hands, clear #1 WR upside. Panthers have a young QB in Bryce Young; chemistry could develop quickly. Rating: Buy — WR1 upside at a reasonable current price point
  • Will Johnson (CB — Pittsburgh Steelers, #20 Overall) — Elite defensive prospect; defensive players rarely command premium RC prices but Will Johnson's profile is exceptional. Rating: Speculative — defensive cards rarely sustain high values long-term

First Topps NFL Rookie Cards — The Historical Premium

We cannot overstate the significance of the licensing context. When Panini took over from Topps for the 2010 NFL season, the first Panini NFL rookie class (2010) has retained enduring collector significance. Sam Bradford's first Panini rookie cards are still reference points for that transition era.

The 2025 Topps NFL rookie class — the first Topps NFL RCs in over 15 years — will be reference cards for a generation. Cam Ward and Travis Hunter's Topps rookie cards are being made at a historic inflection point. That context adds durable collector value independent of on-field performance.

Which Products to Buy for NFL Rookies

  • Topps NFL Series 1 (Base set RC) — The first official Topps NFL rookie card for each player; historically the most collected
  • Topps Chrome NFL — Expected late 2025/early 2026; the chromium refractor version will be the premium RC collectors covet most
  • Topps Finest NFL — On-card autograph product; Cam Ward and Travis Hunter autos from Finest will be the top individual cards of the class
  • Topps Prizm Draft Picks — Pre-draft collegiate cards available before the NFL products; often the first chromium card of a prospect available to collectors
"Travis Hunter is the most interesting card investment story of the 2025 class. A true two-way player in the modern NFL hasn't existed in decades — if he delivers on both sides of the ball, his rookie cards will be among the most unique collectibles in football card history." — James Mitchell, ACT Collectibles

Timing Your Purchases

NFL rookie card prices follow a predictable pattern through the first season:

  • Pre-draft (March–April) — Lowest prices; highest risk. Buy here only if you have strong conviction on a prospect.
  • Draft night spike — Prices jump immediately on selection; avoid buying into the frenzy
  • Training camp dip (June–July) — Prices often soften; good re-entry point
  • Week 1–4 of regular season — Best buying window for players who start strong; before full hype is priced in
  • Playoff run — Sell into the peak; prices spike during playoff games and correct after elimination
